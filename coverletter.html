Cover Letter

Posted by Anze Wang on November 7, 2015

Dear UWP instructor,

Before this quarter, I felt fear to write long essays. However, through this quarter, I have learned lots of method and technique to write essay. As I get used to these methods, I am not afraid to work on hard writing assignment. In this letter, I would talk about how I improve my writing by taking this course.

First, I would like to talk about the conceptual knowledge learned from this course. I understand writing process which is a guideline for me to build up my essay. The thinking process help me prepare a lot before writing. In previous writing course, I used to begin writing as soon as I get the writing task. Because I did not do enough brainstorming, I always stuck in the middle of writing. That’s one of the biggest reason that I do not like write long essay. However, if I prepare some ideas for the essay, I can finish the first draft fluently. Although I still need a significant effort to revise the first draft, it is a substantial improvement for me. Besides the writing process, the rhetorical analysis not only helps me analysis the source article, but also help me make strong, audience-forced essay. In the rest of this letter, I will talk about how I did the writing assignment by apply these skills.

The first assignment is a personal narrative. In order to write this writing task, I first did a brainstorming. I came up several events that influenced me a lot, such as learning physics, preparing for study abroad, and practicing kendo. However, I finally decided to write something about learning competitive programming, because this is the most important things I did in past six years. I learned a lot through the way of practicing competitive programming. After deciding the main topic, I start a new session of brainstorming. There are lots of stories happened during these years. I use a long time to filter some stories which are worth to tell. One is about self-studying. In Chinese high school, teachers prefer to push lots of knowledge into students’ mind, but they never give students chance to learn something by themselves. I learned to study by myself through learning competitive programming. That is a key skill which helps me to survive in college. The other one is about the discouragement from my teachers. I want to write about it because it is the most common events which every Chinese student who have learned competitive programming must face. Because I have two stories in my narrative, my structure is very clear. I first introduce the background of learning competitive programming. And then, I write about two stories separately. Finally, I summarize the whole essay. After this short brainstorming, I began to write the essay. Honestly, my first draft looks terrible. I lost a branch of details and forgot to explain some concept about OI or Gaokao. Fortunately, during the pear review part, my partners throw out some questions about my first draft. Thanks to these question, I began to think about my essay and add lots of detail into it. By doing this assignment, I experienced all of steps of writing process. I think that this assignment largely helped me understand the concept about writing process.

The second assignment is a research paper. It is the most interesting homework in this quarter. Because the assignment did not limit the main topic, we could write whatever we want. However, this freedom made me feel worry about how to choose topics. Because I am a computer science major, I finally decided to write something related to my major. By doing brain storming, I got lots of interesting topics. By considering the audience, who may not have previous knowledge about computer science, I decided to discuss one of the most popular programming languages, Rust. Although these is an easy topic, I still need to introduce some basic knowledge of programming language. Fortunately, I just took programming language last quarter, I can easily apply the knowledge I learned from professor Su in this paper. For the structure of the paper, I divided it in to three parts. The first part talks about the definition of safety. In order to make readers understand that why Rust’s safety is the most important part of it, they must know what is safety for programming language. In the next part, I introduce the advantages of Rust. Although I only introduce some properties of Rust, they are sufficient enough to prove that Rust is a powerful language. In the third part, I show tradeoffs of Rust. It is hard to learn. However, it helps me show that the Rust community is helpful. I think this is a good counter example for this paper. To make this paper easy to understand is the hardest part in this paper. Because it has many difficult computer science concepts. While I revised the paper, I asked some of my friends, who are not computer science major, to read this paper. He told me the points which I did not explain clearly and helped me add more back ground information. Some of the concepts in this paper is trivial for the computer science majors; However, they are strange for the people never heard about them. So, it is necessary to have a friend who majors in different subject. The peer review also helped me fix this problem. However, I did not get clear feedback about which specific points confuse them. They remind me to add more background information into this paper.

In this course, I have learned lots of skills about writing process, rhetorical situation and researching which help improve a lot in my academic writing. Understanding the writing process helps me got a guide line to write academically. Furthermore, the skill to analyze the rhetorical situation teaches me how to appeal my thinking effectively. Lastly, the research skill helps me know how to construct a research and clarify the trustiness of sources. Definitely, the skills I learned in this course can help me be academically successful in the future courses. Thank you for offering the fantastic course and give me a chance to learn these useful skills about writing. I hope you can enjoy rest of contents of the portfolio.

Sincerely,
Anze Wang
